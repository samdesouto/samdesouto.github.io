Fruit Fly Lab
Vendor Commission

â“˜   I was commissioned and eventually became a vendor for the Maimon Lab of Rockefeller University for their scientific research: using fruit flies to study brain functionality. The head of the lab requested me to make video content that shows a viewer-friendly version of the experiments performed for talks and scientific journals/newsletters. Since this was a single-person project, all aspects had to be created by myself, including animation, environments, models, and composition planning.

The first set of requests were done in 2023, which includes creation of all of the meshes/rigs. Fruit fly references were supplied by the lab to aid in modeling and rigging. The model uses both IK and FK rigging, and all bones are seperated into groups. Since the legs have more than two bends, multiple bones are used for full control while retaining the IK rig. There are additional mesh deformation bones used to prevent clipping and add more motion.
