â“˜   This page is a collection of showcased animations put together from Fall 2023. All videos shown are based off of the Five Nights at Freddy's series created by Scott Cawthon. Most of the animations revolve around later games in the series that were also developed by Steel Wool Studios.

The first two videos make up parts of "Freddy's Existential Crisis", a personal project made to practice cinematic animation, based on the game Five Nights at Freddy's: Security Breach. Freddy (left video), an animatronic that is part of a large entertainment mall known as the "Pizzaplex," finds the young protaganist, Gregory (right video), trapped in the mall after hours. Together, they explore the Pizzaplex to find a way out. Late into the night, the pair finds themselves deep underground where animatronics are created and their AI "trained" to be used in this Pizzaplex and others. Only a set of voice lines are played while the player explores this area, but the lines' intense implications felt worthy enough of a cinematic cutscene. While not completely finished, I've shown some shots that were finalized.

The next two videos are parts of "Foxy's Repair: Remake", based off of the game Five Nights at Freddy's: Help Wanted. This VR game is filled with dozens of horror "minigames" that expand the flat-screen-platform experiences of previous FNAF games. "Foxy's Repair" is one of these minigames; you are tasked with repairing Foxy, a broken animatronic, while avoiding his dangerous movements.

Because Help Wanted was Steel Wool's first inclusion into FNAF, the animation would usually feel underwhelming until the studio grew alongside more releases. As a result, Foxy's animations in his level are very linear and simple. I took it upon myself as a fun challenge to recreate these animations, giving the animatronic a more "weighted" feel and exploring its use of layered animations. The left video shows all of my animations properly combined, with the right video showing a more detailed breakdown of those seperate portions animated. All animations can be easily layered in a game engine such as Unreal Engine.

The final video, "The Monster is Back", is a silly joke experiment on implementing 2D environments in 3D software, but it turned out surprisingly great. It's based on an end-game cutscene from Five Nights at Freddy's: Sister Location, where the mangled, sleep-deprived, animatronic-ridden protagonist makes his way home from horror-filled nights at an underground animatronic storage location. A mix of rigs, drivers, and shapekeys allow me to manipulate the assets while keeping it 2D.

The most unique aspect this particular animation is that only the first scene is accurate to what is already in-game. The opposite view is nonexistent, and the figures never actually speak in-game. Those styles were concepted by myself.
