The first two videos are part of a personal project, "Sister Location Trailer: Remastered". The project is a personal reinterpretation of the original game trailer for Five Nights at Freddy's: Sister Location. While it was not the first time I had explored robotic and momentum-based animation, this was the moment where I had mastered it.

The third video was an experiment with creating loopable taunts (with extras), using a fan-concept for the Roblox fan-game Arsenal. The taunt features the "Kazotsky Kick" ("Russian Kick"), and mimics a similar taunt used in popular first-person shooter Team Fortress 2.

The final three videos are loopable concepts for characters found throughout the Five Nights at Freddy's series.

On a slight tangent... the first, second, and fourth videos are some of my last animated works using Source Filmmaker (a free-to-use animation program for Valve games) before I moved on to more advanced programs such as Blender. Source Filmmaker (SFM) allows advanced animation, lighting, and rendering, but there is no way to directly models, rigs, and materials. These assets would have to be made outside of the program with third-party tools. This makes SFM very easy to pick up and learn, but hard to personalize due to limited assets. It was the first animation program I used, and though it is basic and platform-specific (and no longer maintained), I've found this program has just as much worth in being used in a professional context as other programs.
