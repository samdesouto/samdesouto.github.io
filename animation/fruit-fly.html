Fruit Fly Lab
Vendor Commission

â“˜   I was commissioned and eventually became a vendor for the Maimon Lab of Rockefeller University for their scientific research: using fruit flies to study brain functionality. The head of the lab requested me to make video content that shows a viewer-friendly version of the experiments performed for talks and scientific journals/newsletters. Since this was a single-person project, all aspects had to be created by myself, including animation, environments, models, and composition planning.

The first set of requests were done in 2023. I first modeled the fly and animated a walking loop for prior approval, which passed after some revisions. Those first three videos are shown on the top.

The video after involved the lab's research on offset orientation and memory of the orientation. The lab would carefully watch the neuron activity of the fruit fly as she aligns herself to the sun despite being occasionally moved off course. An introductionary animation was also requested as an engaging backstory, shown in the very last video.

The lab reached out to me once again in early 2025 to illustrate another topic expanded from their previous research. It now included a direction incentive in the form of fruit-scented wind. The fly would remember the upwind direction compared to the sun's location, even if the wind ceased. This is shown in the second video.

Not were many improvements made in this version, but a new animation driver was created for dynamic ground movement. For ease of animation, the fly is staying in one place, but the ground displaces its material based on angle and intensity inputs, taking advantage of Blender's new Geometry Nodes.
